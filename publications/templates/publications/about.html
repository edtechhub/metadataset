Interactive evidence synthesis

How should evidence be used to make decisions, if the evidence is synthesized at a global scale, across many different contexts, but the decisions are made at a local scale, in a context that differs from the global average?

This website enables users to interact with a global evidence base, to find evidence that is relevant to their decisions. As well as browsing our evidence atlases to find publications on a specific intervention, outcome, or country, we are also developing a method of interactive evidence synthesis that we call "dynamic meta-analysis".

Dynamic meta-analysis

Dynamic meta-analysis is our method of improving the transferability of evidence to a specific context. This website provides two methods for this: (1) filtering, in which studies can be excluded from a meta-analysis if they are not relevant to that context ("subgroup analysis"), and (2) weighting, in which studies can be given a lower weight if they are less relevant to that context ("recalibration") (please see Kneale et al. (2019) for information on transferability and recalibration).

To see how a dynamic meta-analysis works, we suggest that you try this analysis of data on cover crops (try filtering studies in which the "cover crop type" is either "legume" or "brassica").

Please note that this is a work in progress.
